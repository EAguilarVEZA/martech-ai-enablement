Three mental models that make everything click

Most people get more out of Claude in a day once these land. Teach these first; every workflow afterward is easier.

1

A prompt is delegation

Brief Claude like a sharp new teammate: who it should act as (role), what you want (goal), the inputs (context), the rules (constraints), and what “done” looks like (format).

2

Context is everything

Claude only knows what you give it. Paste the notes, attach the file, name the audience. Vague in, vague out — specific in, useful out.

3

You own verification

Claude drafts; you decide. Treat output as a fast first draft, check facts and numbers, and keep a human review before anything goes external.

From chat to agent: where are you on the ladder?

People learn faster when they can see the next rung. Find yourself, then aim one step up.

Rung 1 · Chat assistant

You can: ask questions, draft and rewrite, brainstorm, summarize. Next step: add role, context, and a format to every prompt (see Foundations).

Rung 2 · Document & data partner

You can: drop in files, images, and spreadsheets and have Claude analyze, extract, and explain. Next step: use Projects to keep shared context so you stop re-explaining.

Rung 3 · Builder

You can: create reusable artifacts, templates, and on-brand designs with Claude Design. Next step: standardize a few team templates and a shared prompt library.

Rung 4 · Connected agent

You can: connect tools, use helper agents, run scheduled work, and hand technical tasks to Claude Code. Next step: automate one recurring workflow end to end.
