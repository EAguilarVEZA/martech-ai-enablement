Power features: Projects, Files & Artifacts

Once people are comfortable in chat, these are the features that make Claude dramatically more useful for a team — shared context, real files, and reusable outputs.

Projects

Stop re-explaining yourself

A Project keeps persistent context, custom instructions, and a small knowledge base in one place, so every chat starts already knowing your brand, audience, and rules. Set it once; the whole team benefits.

Try: a “Campaign Brief Project” with your template, tone, and approval checklist preloaded.

Files & vision

Bring your real material

Drop in a PDF, spreadsheet, screenshot, or chart and ask Claude to extract, summarize, compare, or analyze it. This is the fastest “aha” for non-technical folks — no copy-paste, just the source.

Try: “Compare these two campaign reports and tell me what changed and why it matters.”

Artifacts

Turn answers into assets

Ask Claude to package a good result as a reusable artifact — a checklist, one-pager, table, tracker, or even a small working web page — that the team can reuse and adapt.

Try: “Turn this into a reusable launch-QA checklist with placeholders.”

Skills & memory

Reusable know-how that sticks

Skills are reusable capabilities you can give Claude for repeatable jobs, and memory lets Claude carry useful team context forward instead of starting cold every time. Together they make Claude feel less like a tool and more like a teammate who already knows how you work.

Try it

Analyze a file

Here is the attached [spreadsheet / PDF / screenshot]. Without inventing anything, give me:
- the 3 most important takeaways
- 2 numbers worth double-checking
- 1 chart or table that would communicate this best
- the next question I should ask
Attach a non-sensitive file first.
Guardrail

Keep it safe

Only put approved, non-sensitive material in Projects, files, and artifacts. Anything that lands in a shared Project is visible to everyone with access — treat it like a shared drive.
