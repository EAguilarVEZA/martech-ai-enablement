Extreme Ownership

Verify your output

The one skill that separates confident Claude users from cautious ones. Claude drafts fast and is often right — but it can be confidently wrong, so you own the final check. Treat every output as a strong first draft, not a source of truth.

Why

Fast, not infallible

AI can invent facts, numbers, or sources that look right (“hallucinations”). The cost of a wrong number in a leadership deck is high; a 30-second check is cheap.

What to check

The five things

Facts and names, numbers and math, citations and sources, the logic of any claim, and anything that will go external or to a stakeholder.

How

Make Claude help you check

Ask it to cite sources, separate facts from assumptions, and flag what it is unsure about. Cross-check key numbers yourself. For high stakes, get a second set of eyes.

Hands-on lesson

Run a verification pass

Use case: You have a Claude draft about to go to a stakeholder and want to catch anything risky before you send it.

Prompt
Review the text below for accuracy before I share it.

Do not rewrite it yet. Instead, list:
- Claims that need a source
- Numbers or dates to double-check
- Anything you are not fully confident about
- Assumptions you made to write it

Text:
[paste the draft]

Try it: Paste a real, non-sensitive draft and use the flag list to decide what to verify before sending.

See an example result
Needs a source: the “industry average open rate of 22%” — confirm against your own data.
Double-check: the Q3 figure (the chart says 18%, the text says 28%).
Low confidence: the claim that mobile drove the lift — it is not in the notes you provided.
Assumption: “leadership” means the VP staff — confirm the audience.
Fix these four and the draft is safe to send.
Checklist

Before you ship

  • Did I verify every number and date?
  • Are claims supported, or did Claude flag them?
  • Are the sources real and checked, not invented?
  • Would I sign my name to this, or email it to a vendor?
  • Did a human review anything high-stakes or external?
Where this fits

The habit behind responsible use

Verification pairs with the team guardrails and the “you own verification” mental model on the home page. AI drafts; people decide.
