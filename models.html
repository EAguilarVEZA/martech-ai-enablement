Extreme Ownership

Choosing a model & thinking time

Claude comes in a few sizes, and you can let it think longer on hard problems. You rarely need to fuss over this — but knowing the trade-off helps you get faster, better results.

Opus

Most capable

The deepest reasoning. Reach for it on the hardest analysis, complex multi-step work, and high-stakes drafts where quality matters most.

Sonnet

The everyday default

Balanced and fast, and strong enough for almost all martech work — briefs, research, content, decks. Start here unless you have a reason not to.

Haiku

Fastest & most economical

Best for quick, simple, high-volume tasks where speed and cost matter more than depth.

If your task is…Reach for
A quick rewrite, summary, or simple answerHaiku (or the default)
Most day-to-day work — briefs, research, content, decksSonnet (the default)
Hard reasoning, complex analysis, or a high-stakes deliverableOpus
Extended thinking

Let it think longer

For genuinely hard, multi-step problems you can let Claude reason for longer before it answers — trading a little speed for noticeably better depth. For simple tasks, skip it; the extra thinking just slows you down.

Rule of thumb

Don't overthink it

Use the default model for almost everything. Switch up to the most capable model only when the answer really matters or the problem is genuinely complex, and turn on longer thinking for those same hard cases.
