Plans Guardrails

Team guardrails

The team should use Claude as a collaborator, not an autopilot. These guardrails give people confidence about what to try, what to verify, when to escalate, and what not to share.

Do
  • Use Claude for drafts, structure, synthesis, alternatives, and QA checks.
  • Include audience, goal, context, constraints, and format in the prompt.
  • Verify facts, data, claims, and source quality before sharing externally.
  • Save useful prompts and examples so the team builds a shared library.
Avoid
  • Do not paste confidential, regulated, customer, or sensitive data unless your company policy allows it.
  • Do not publish Claude output without human review and brand/legal checks.
  • Do not ask for “make it better” without saying better for whom and why.
  • Do not treat a polished answer as a verified answer.
Data

Protect sensitive information

  • Use anonymized or synthetic examples when practicing.
  • Remove customer names, emails, account IDs, contract details, and personal data unless policy explicitly allows it.
  • Do not paste secrets, API keys, credentials, private URLs, unpublished financials, or restricted partner data.
Accuracy

Verify before sharing

  • Check facts, dates, benchmarks, pricing, claims, and source quality.
  • Ask Claude to separate facts, assumptions, and recommendations.
  • For external content, require human brand, legal, and stakeholder review.
Brand

Keep voice and claims aligned

  • Provide approved messaging, audience, tone, and forbidden claims.
  • Ask Claude to flag claims that need evidence or approval.
  • Do not let Claude invent customer quotes, performance metrics, or product capabilities.
Research

Demand traceability

  • Ask for sources, caveats, and confidence level.
  • Do not mix sourced facts with Claude’s guesses.
  • Use research summaries as decision support, not final truth.
Automation

Keep humans in the loop

  • Review outputs before sending, publishing, uploading, or changing systems.
  • For Cowork and Code, ask Claude to show what files it read or changed.
  • Use approval checkpoints before any action with business impact.
Technical work

Plan, test, and preserve

  • Ask Claude Code to explore first and wait for approval before edits.
  • Require checks: tests, lint, screenshots, tracking validation, or sample output.
  • Do not allow broad destructive actions without clear scope and backup.
Collaboration

Make learning visible

  • Share useful prompts, failed prompts, and before/after examples.
  • Create a common prompt library for repeatable martech workflows.
  • Capture unresolved questions in the Q&A board and answer them after the session.
Escalation

Know when to stop

  • Escalate if the request involves legal, security, procurement, regulated data, or customer-impacting decisions.
  • Pause if Claude contradicts known facts or cannot cite sources.
  • Ask an owner to approve before changing templates, tracking, automations, or live content.
Red flags

Watch for risky patterns

  • “Just send it as-is.”
  • “Use this customer data; it is probably fine.”
  • “Make up a benchmark.”
  • “Change the live workflow without telling anyone.”
