OH MarTech AI Challenge

Turn the learning program into a fun innovation sprint for Orlando Health martech. Anyone can register, join a track, submit an idea, and collaborate on a practical AI-enabled improvement that helps the team work smarter.

Purpose

Learn by building

Use AI on a small, useful martech problem instead of only watching demos.

Format

2-week sprint

Form small cross-functional teams, prototype, share lessons, and recommend next steps.

Output

Working concept

Submit a prompt, artifact, workflow, dashboard, content system, or lightweight technical prototype.

Recognition

Showcase day

Celebrate practical ideas, responsible AI use, and collaboration across skill levels.

Step 1 · Sign up

Join the challenge

Use approved, non-sensitive examples only. Your sign-up and progress save in your browser.

Step 2 · Your assignment

Sign up on the left and you'll get your track and a five-step task checklist to make your challenge real.

Step 3 · Progress board

Who's in and how far they've got

Track 1

Website and digital experience

Examples: landing page QA assistant, accessibility checklist, SEO brief generator, content-to-page outline workflow.

Track 2

Content and campaign strategy

Examples: campaign narrative builder, audience message matrix, service-line content repurposing workflow.

Track 3

Analytics and insights

Examples: weekly performance summary, experiment readout template, anomaly explanation assistant.

Track 4

Operations and automation

Examples: launch readiness board, UTM QA checklist, intake triage helper, approval handoff tracker.

Track 5

Technical innovation

Examples: CSV cleanup script, tracking audit helper, prototype app, prompt library search, lightweight dashboard.

Judging criteria

How ideas are selected

  • Useful for Orlando Health martech work
  • Safe and responsible with data
  • Clear owner, workflow, and adoption path
  • Cross-functional collaboration
  • Evidence that the prototype improves speed, quality, or consistency
Challenge rhythm

Suggested sprint flow

  • Day 1: kickoff, tracks, guardrails, team formation
  • Days 2-5: problem framing, examples, prompt/prototype work
  • Days 6-8: test with real but safe scenarios
  • Day 9: polish the story and evidence
  • Day 10: showcase, feedback, and next-step decision
Team mix

Who should join

  • Website and digital experience teammates
  • Content, campaign, and brand writers
  • Analytics, insights, and reporting partners
  • Marketing operations and automation owners
  • Technical innovators who can prototype safely
